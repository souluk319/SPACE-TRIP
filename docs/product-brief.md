# Space Journey Edu Product Brief

## Problem

Many educational space pages show impressive planets, stars, and galaxies, but children often leave with only a vague feeling that "space is big."

That is not enough.

The product should help children build a memorable scale model:

```text
Earth -> Moon -> Solar System -> stars -> galaxy
```

## Product concept

Space Journey Edu is a guided interactive web journey where a child travels from Earth to galaxy scale through four short stages.

It uses visual scale, simple missions, and a friendly guide character to make abstract space distances easier to feel.

## Target experience

1. Child opens the app.
2. Taps `Start Journey`.
3. A guide character explains the first mission.
4. The child moves through four stages.
5. Each stage teaches one concept.
6. The child answers small checkpoints.
7. The app ends with a completion card.

## Product principles

1. Learning beats spectacle.
2. One concept per stage.
3. Short Korean sentences.
4. Guided journey before free exploration.
5. Missions beat passive reading.
6. Visual scale should create an "aha" moment.
7. The app should work on mobile.

## Non-goals for MVP

- No login.
- No backend.
- No account system.
- No open-world simulator.
- No large encyclopedia.
- No long text panels.
- No advanced physics engine.
- No complex orbital simulation.

## Stage design

### Stage 1 - Earth and Moon

Learning concept:

- The Moon looks close, but it is far from Earth.

Mission:

- Travel from Earth to the Moon.

Guide line:

- `달은 가까워 보이지만, 지구에서 아주 멀리 떨어져 있어.`

Checkpoint:

- `달은 지구보다 클까, 작을까?`

### Stage 2 - Solar System

Learning concept:

- Planets orbit the Sun, and the distances between them are huge.

Mission:

- Find Mars and Jupiter.

Guide line:

- `태양은 우리 태양계의 중심이고, 행성들은 태양 주변을 돌아.`

Checkpoint:

- `태양계에서 가장 큰 행성은 무엇일까?`

### Stage 3 - Beyond the Solar System

Learning concept:

- Voyager 1 is very far away, but still tiny compared to the distance to nearby stars.

Mission:

- Find Voyager 1, then look toward the nearest star.

Guide line:

- `사람이 만든 탐사선도 멀리 갔지만, 별까지의 거리는 훨씬 더 커.`

Checkpoint:

- `태양도 별일까?`

### Stage 4 - Milky Way and Galaxies

Learning concept:

- A galaxy is a huge collection of stars.

Mission:

- Leave the Solar System and see the Milky Way.

Guide line:

- `우리은하에는 태양 같은 별이 아주 많이 모여 있어.`

Checkpoint:

- `은하는 별 하나일까, 별들의 모임일까?`

## Completion moment

Show a completion card:

```text
오늘의 우주여행 완료
지구에서 출발해 달, 태양계, 별, 은하까지 여행했어요.
```

Optional share card later:

- Child name or nickname
- Completed stages
- Favorite stage
- One learned fact

## Voice and tone

Korean.

Tone:

- Warm
- Curious
- Short
- Child-friendly

Avoid:

- Dense scientific paragraphs
- Adult technical explanations
- Too many numbers at once

Good copy:

- `준비됐어? 지구에서 출발해보자.`
- `달까지 가는 길은 생각보다 길어.`
- `우와, 이제 태양계가 보이기 시작했어.`
- `태양도 사실은 별이야.`

## Visual direction

Use real space inspiration, but keep the learning path clear.

Recommended:

- Dark space background
- Clear object labels
- Warm guide character UI
- Simple glowing path line
- Stage-based zoom transitions
- Large readable Korean text

Avoid:

- Too many small objects
- Text floating everywhere
- Overly realistic scale that makes the app unusable
- Controls that require explanation

## Success criteria

- A child can start without help.
- The full journey finishes in under 5 minutes.
- Each stage has one clear mission.
- Each stage teaches one clear concept.
- A parent can understand the educational value quickly.
- The app works on a phone browser.

## Current risk

The prototype can become a pretty space viewer instead of an educational journey.

The next build prompt must prioritize lesson flow, missions, and checkpoints over visual complexity.
